✨ Starters

Punugulu

Little crunchy balls made from dosa batter that are fun to bite

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
1 cup dosa/idli batter, salt, cumin seeds, chopped onion/chilli (optional), oil.

Steps:
1.Mix batter, salt, and cumin (add onion/chilli if you like)
2.Heat oil; drop small dollops of batter.
3.Fry until golden and crisp.
4.Drain and eat with chutney.

Pesarattu

A green pancake made from moong beans—soft on the inside, crisp at the edges

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
Soaked moong beans, salt, ginger, green chilli, oil.

Steps:
1.Grind beans with ginger, chilli, salt to make batter.
2.Spread thin on a hot pan.
3. Drizzle oil; cook until you see golden spots.
4.Fold and eat with chutney.

🍛 Main Course

Gongura Mutton

Tender meat cooked with sour sorrel leaves—tangy and yummy.

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
mutton, gongura leaves, onion, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chilli powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, whole spices

Steps:
1.Cook mutton with ginger-garlic, onion, chilli, turmeric, and salt until soft.
2.Grind masala spices (e.g. cumin, coriander, garlic) and add.
3.Stir in chopped gongura leaves, cook till oil surfaces.
4.Simmer a few minutes and serve hot with rice.

Pulihora (Tamarind Rice)

Rice mixed with tamarind, spices, and peanuts—sweet and sour comfort.

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
cooked rice, tamarind extract, peanuts, mustard seeds, chana dal, urad dal, dried chillies, curry leaves, turmeric, chilli powder, salt, oil.

Steps:
1.Fry mustard, chana/urad dal, chillies, curry leaves in oil.
2.Add tamarind extract, turmeric, chilli powder, salt.
3.Mix this into cooked rice well.
4.Serve warm or pack for lunch.

Gutthi Vankaya Kura

Little eggplants stuffed with spicy peanut paste—melt-in-your-mouth.

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
small brinjals, peanuts, sesame seeds (and optional coconut), chilli powder, coriander powder, tamarind, salt, oil, mustard seeds, curry leaves

Steps:
1.Grind peanuts, sesame (and coconut) with spices into paste.
2.Slit brinjals, stuff with paste.
3.Sauté mustard seeds, curry leaves, then cook stuffed eggplants.
4.Add tamarind water, simmer until soft.

Pootharekulu

Paper-thin rice rolls with sweet jaggery—like candy clouds.

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
rice paper sheets, ghee, powdered jaggery or sugar

Steps:
1.Lay a paper-thin rice sheet on a plate.

Ariselu

Sweet, flat rice-jaggery discs—soft and chewy

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
rice flour, jaggery, ghee/oil

Steps:
1.Mix rice flour and jaggery into soft dough.
2.Flatten small discs.
3.Fry discs in hot oil until golden.
4.Drain and cool before eating.

Kakinada Khaja

Flaky deep-fried pastry soaked in sweet syrup—crunchy and juicy

Click to view recipe

Recipe:

Ingredients:
all-purpose flour-based layered dough, ghee (in layers), sugar syrup

Steps:
1.Make layered dough with ghee between.
2.Cut into shapes.
3.Fry until crisp and brown.
4. Dip in sugar syrup and drain.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Are these traditional recipes?

Yes, all recipes are rooted in authentic Andhra cuisine with some modern tweaks for ease of preparation in today's kitchens.

Are measurements provided?

This version showcases dishes visually. For detailed measurements and step-by-step instructions, future updates will include printable recipe cards.

Can I substitute ingredients?

Many ingredients can be substituted based on availability and dietary preferences. We'll provide alternative options in detailed recipe cards.

How spicy are these dishes?

Andhra cuisine is known for its bold flavors and spice levels. All recipes can be adjusted to your preferred spice level.
